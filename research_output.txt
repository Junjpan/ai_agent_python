Summary:
Fiji, officially the Republic of Fiji, is an island country located in Melanesia, part of Oceania in the South Pacific Ocean. It is situated approximately 1,100 nautical miles (2,000 km; 1,300 mi) north-northeast of New Zealand. The country consists of an extensive archipelago comprising more than 330 islands, of which approximately 110 are permanently inhabited, along with more than 500 islets. The total land area amounts to about 18,300 square kilometres (7,100 sq mi). The most outlying island group is Ono-i-Lau. About 87% of the total population resides in the country.
